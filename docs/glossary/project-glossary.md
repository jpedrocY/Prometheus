# Project Glossary

## Alpha
A repeatable source of excess return or edge.

## Backtest
A historical simulation of strategy behavior under defined assumptions.

## Shadow Trading
Generating live order intents without sending them to the exchange matching engine.

## Paper Trading
Simulated trading using modeled fills.

## Isolated Margin
Margin allocated to a single position or symbol context rather than shared across the account.

## Cross Margin
Margin shared across positions within the account.

## One-Way Mode
Single net directional position per symbol.

## Hedge Mode
Ability to maintain long and short exposure simultaneously on the same symbol.

## Liquidation
Forced closure when maintenance requirements are breached.

## Drawdown
Peak-to-trough decline in equity or account value.

## Slippage
Difference between expected execution price and actual execution price.

## FVG
Fair value gap; generally a short-term imbalance concept requiring project-specific formalization.

## Regime
A market condition class such as trending, ranging, volatile, or mean-reverting.

## Reconciliation
The process of aligning internal system state with exchange-reported actual state.

## Kill Switch
A mechanism that halts or sharply restricts trading under predefined conditions.

## Event-Driven Architecture
A system design in which components react to events such as market updates, fills, or state changes.

## Operator Console
The future interface layer used for visibility, review, and limited manual actions.

## ADR
Architecture Decision Record; a document capturing a significant decision and its rationale.
